As more New Yorkers show symptoms of the coronavirus, Healthier is offering this guidance for those who think they have been exposed. If you suspect you have COVID-19, or another virus, please follow these steps to safely monitor your symptoms at home, know when to seek care, and know the guidance for who should be tested.

─Unless you are severely sick, avoid going to the Emergency Room.

or Doctor’s Office to limit exposure to other patients and health care workers. If you feel sick, stay home and call your health care provider



─If you have symptoms for more than 3 or 4 days, call 311

If needed, you will be connected to a clinician who will help determine if you need to seek treatment, or if you should stay home and continue to monitor your symptoms to prevent exposure to others.


─self-isolate or schedule a time for further evaluation

If you may have come in contact with a person who is sick, you will likely be asked to self-isolate or schedule a time for further evaluation. Most cases of the virus involve mild and moderate symptoms of fever and cough and can be safely managed at home.


─Call 911 is you are having trouble breathing

Call 911 is you are having trouble breathing or any other symptoms that require immediate medical attention.


If You Have a Scheduled Appointment

We encourage all New Yorkers, particularly the elderly and people with chronic health conditions, to stay home, limit travel and exposure to the virus. healthier is postponing most elective surgeries and procedures. If you have a scheduled appointment for a routine check-up or other non-emergency care, we encourage you to have a telephonic visit with a provider. Contact your healthier provider directly or call us at 1-844-NYC-4NYC (1-844-692-4692).
